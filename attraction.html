Main Attractions of Aluva

Aluva Metro

Aluva Metro Aluva Metro Station is a key part of the Kochi Metro Rail network.
Location: Aluva Metro Station is situated in the heart of Aluva, along the NH 66, near the Aluva flyover and the banks of the Periyar River.
Facilities: Spread over an area of 1.5 lakh square feet, it is the second-largest station in the Kochi Metro network after Vadakkekota. The station is equipped with modern amenities, including ticket counters, automated ticketing machines, elevators, and restrooms.
Accessibility: The station is well-connected to various parts of Aluva and Kochi, making it convenient for commuters and tourists alike.
Operating Hours: The first passenger service from Aluva starts at 6:00 AM, and the last service is at 10:30 PM on weekdays, with slightly extended hours on weekends


Aqueduct Bridge

The Aqueduct Bridge in Aluva, also known as the Premam Bridge, is a well-known landmark in Aluva, Kerala.
Location: The bridge is situated across the Periyar River in Aluva, Kochi.
History: Originally constructed to transport water from Bhoothathankettu to Paravoor for agricultural purposes, the aqueduct has since been abandoned.
Popularity: The bridge gained widespread fame after being featured in the Malayalam movie "Premam" starring Nivin Pauly.
Scenic Views: The bridge offers picturesque views of the Periyar River and the surrounding areas.
Walkway: It was a popular spot for walks, especially during sunrise and sunset


Aluva Temple

The Aluva Mahadeva Temple, is a renowned Hindu temple dedicated to Lord Shiva, located on the banks of the Periyar River in Aluva, Kerala.
Location: Aluva Mahadeva Temple is situated in Aluva Manappuram, on the banks of the Periyar River, in suburban Kochi.
Deity: The presiding deity of the temple is Lord Shiva, enshrined in the main Sanctum Sanctorum facing east.
Legend: According to folklore, the idol of Lord Shiva was installed by the sage Parashurama.

Festivals
Aluva Sivarathri: The temple is most famous for the Aluva Sivarathri festival, which is celebrated annually in the Malayalam month of Karkidakam (February-March).
Devotees stay awake all night in prayer, and a dip in the river after the rituals is considered very sacred2.

Architecture
Style: The temple is built in the traditional Kerala style of architecture.
